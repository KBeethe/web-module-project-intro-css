Mueller State Park has over 5,000 acres of spring-fed meadows, forested ridges and massive rocks of Pikes Peak Granite with several ponds that dot the landscape. The park supports a variety of plant-life ranging from native grasses and wildflowers to stands of spruce, fir, pine and aspen.

This beautiful state park situated near Colorado Springs is a playground for outdoor adventurers with miles of trails, 5,000 acres of forested mountains, and a campsite for overnight guests. In the winter this area is great for snowshoeing, cross-country skiing, and winter camping.

We strongly recommend that you make reservations for lodging, camping, and backpacking. You can pay the park entrance fee upon arrival (there's no need to pay it in advance).
